## 1) Vision & User Experience

### What makes this novel
Instead of classic forward simulation (“if I change X, what happens?”), the dashboard supports **inverse design**: users state **policy goals with constraints**, and the system searches the policy/assumption space to propose **feasible bundles** (e.g., fiscal stance, rate path assumptions, energy price scenarios) that most likely achieve those targets—**with plain‑language rationales** and quantified confidence. The RL agent is bounded by realistic constraints (e.g., policy change limits, plausibility bands) and learns which combinations steer the economic system toward the goal.

**Example prompt in the dashboard:**  
> “Get CPI inflation near **2.5% by Q4** while **unemployment ≥ 4%**, keep **GDP growth ≥ 1%**, and **no negative quarterly real wage growth**.”

The system returns:
- **Bundle A:** Tighten policy rate path modestly (+75 bps over 3 quarters), maintain fiscal deficit at ~3% of GDP, moderate energy price assumptions (+5%), USD index +2% range.  
- **Bundle B:** Smaller rate increase (+25 bps), temporary supply‑side tax credits, stricter credit conditions (loan spreads +30 bp), neutral energy assumptions.  
Each bundle includes **forecast deltas**, **risk bands**, **trade‑offs** (e.g., slower investment vs. lower inflation), and **explanations** in everyday language generated by Bedrock LLMs.

## 2) Data & Ingestion

### Sources
- **FRED** for canonical U.S. macro series (CPI, unemployment, GDP, yields, credit spreads, USD index, etc.) via the public API (supporting series observations, releases, vintages).   
- **BEA** for detailed GDP/NIPA tables (e.g., PCE components, GDP by industry), accessed via BEA’s developer API (JSON/XML) and official tooling. 

### Ingestion & storage
- **AWS Glue** jobs (scheduled) pull **FRED** endpoints (`series/observations`, `releases`, `vintagedates`) and **BEA** datasets (NIPA, GDP by Industry) into raw **Amazon S3** zones (Bronze). 
- **AWS Glue/Spark** transforms normalize frequency (monthly/quarterly), align calendars, track vintages (ALFRED concept) to support “what‑was‑known‑when” analysis. (FRED provides vintage dates for revision tracking.) 
- **AWS Glue Data Catalog** + **Athena** expose curated (Silver/Gold) tables for downstream modeling and the dashboard. (Standard AWS analytics pattern; QuickSight connects natively.) 

> **Note on BEA usage**: BEA’s API requires a free key; official `beaapi` references describe datasets & parameters (e.g., `NIPA`, frequency flags).

## 3) Forecasting Engine (Short‑Term Outlook)

### Modeling choices
- **Time series forecasting with DeepAR on Amazon SageMaker** (built‑in algorithm) for multivariate macro targets (CPI components, unemployment, GDP, yields), leveraging shared patterns across many related series. DeepAR is designed for probabilistic forecasts across multiple related time series and typically outperforms ARIMA/ETS in such contexts. 
- Alternatively (if you prefer the managed service): **Amazon Forecast (DeepAR+)**—though note **Amazon Forecast is no longer available to new customers**; for hackathon reliability, use **SageMaker DeepAR**. 

### Feature design
- **Static features**: categorical flags (industry, component, region). **Dynamic features**: policy rate path assumptions, credit spreads, energy prices, USD index, and exogenous policy levers used later by the RL agent (DeepAR supports dynamic known‑future features—handy for this). 
- **Probabilistic outputs**: quantiles (e.g., P10/P50/P90) to feed risk bands and RL reward signals.

### Explainability
- **SageMaker Clarify** produces **SHAP feature attributions** (local & global) per forecasted variable, enabling **plain‑language rationale** and drift monitoring over time. Clarify implements Shapley‑value explainability adapted to ML contexts.

## 4) Policy Path‑Finder: Constrained RL + GenAI Reasoner

### Problem framing
We define an episodic RL environment where **actions** are **policy/assumption levers** (e.g., rate path increments, fiscal stance, energy price scenario, USD index bounds, credit conditions). **State** includes latest economic indicator vectors + latent conditions. **Transition** uses the **forecasting model** as a dynamics oracle: given current state and an action (policy bundle), the DeepAR‑based simulator produces **next‑period distributions** for the target indicators.

### Constraints & feasibility
- **Action bounds**: realistic policy increments (e.g., max ±100 bp per quarter for the policy rate), fiscal constraints (deficit/GDP bands), plausibility flags for exogenous assumptions.
- **Goal & penalties**: reward is high when end‑of‑horizon targets are met (e.g., CPI Q4 ≈ 2.5%) and constraints satisfied (unemployment ≥ 4%, GDP ≥ 1%, wage growth non‑negative). Penalize volatility or excessive policy moves.

### Search strategy
- **Constrained RL policy optimization** (e.g., soft actor‑critic with Lagrangian constraint handling) or **goal‑conditioned planning** (model‑based sampling of action sequences) evaluated against the forecast oracle. We cache promising bundles and their **risk bands** using the probabilistic outputs.  
- The system ranks bundles by **feasibility** (probability of satisfying all goals), **cost** (sum of policy moves), and **robustness** (sensitivity to shocks).

### Human‑readable reasoning
- After the RL proposes bundles, a **Bedrock LLM** (e.g., Amazon **Nova** family) generates **plain‑language explanations**: *“Bundle A leans on a moderately tighter rate path; it tempers PCE inflation via slower credit growth while preserving employment because fiscal remains accommodative…”*  
- The LLM is orchestrated via **Amazon Bedrock** with **Guardrails** and **Prompt management**, and can be augmented by a **RAG** layer that retrieves relevant SHAP summaries and historical analogs from an **OpenSearch** vector store populated with Titan embeddings. Bedrock provides managed access to foundation models (including Nova & Titan) and tooling for guardrails, knowledge bases, and flows.

## 5) Retrieval & Explanation Enhancement (RAG)

To ground explanations in data:
- Chunk Clarify reports, forecast diagnostics, and relevant BEA/FRED metadata; embed with **Amazon Titan Text Embeddings** (including **binary embeddings** for cost‑effective storage). Store vectors in **Amazon OpenSearch Serverless** (supports FP32/FP16 and **binary vectors**) for semantic retrieval. 
- **Semantic search** patterns between Bedrock Titan embeddings and OpenSearch are documented by OpenSearch; connectors/roles allow Bedrock invocation for embeddings. 

This ensures the GenAI explanation references concrete evidence (e.g., “global feature importance shows energy prices and rate path explain 63% of CPI variance in Q3”), improving trust.

### Visual fabric

## 7) Alerts & Monitoring

### Significant deviations
- When actuals or forecasts deviate beyond thresholds (e.g., CPI surprise > 0.5 pp vs. baseline), **SageMaker Model Monitor + Clarify** track **feature attribution drift** and **bias drift** (for fairness in any downstream ML decisions). Configure alerts in **CloudWatch**. 
- **Eventing**: Use **Amazon EventBridge** rules to trigger **SNS** notifications (email/Teams/Slack via Amazon Q Developer in chat applications) for deviations or data ingestion failures. EventBridge → SNS tutorials show rule creation and notification flow; custom subjects possible via Lambda.

## 9) Component Design Details

### A) Data Pipeline
- **Orchestration**: AWS Glue jobs scheduled daily/weekly per series; maintain **versioned S3** buckets to track revisions (via vintage dates) for “as‑of” analysis. FRED exposes endpoints for series and vintages; BEA API exposes datasets and parameter schemas. 
- **Quality gates**: Glue jobs produce **Athena** queries for schema validation (missing periods, duplicates, unit consistency).

### B) Forecast Service
- **Training**: SageMaker built‑in DeepAR; define `context_length`, `prediction_length`, dynamic features for policy levers. (DeepAR hyperparameters & data channels documented.) 
- **Deployment**: Real‑time endpoint (or batch transforms) returns quantile forecasts for selected series.

### C) RL Environment
- **Simulator**: For a candidate policy bundle (action sequence), roll the DeepAR forecasts forward to compute outcome distribution at horizon T (e.g., Q4).  
- **Reward**: high if CPI≈target ± tolerance AND constraints satisfied; include penalties for large policy swings.  
- **Search**: Use constrained policy optimization or sampling over discrete grids + local gradient‑free refinement. Cache results per starting state to speed interaction.

### D) GenAI Explanations (Bedrock)
- **Prompt template**: Provide target, bundles, SHAP summaries, historical analogs (retrieved via **OpenSearch** RAG), and ask the LLM to explain *why this bundle is likely to work and what risks remain* in **plain language**. Bedrock user guide outlines inference, guardrails, prompt management, and knowledge bases; model support lists Nova variants and Titan embeddings. 
- **Guardrails**: Ensure the model stays on topic and avoids unsupported claims; ground content with retrieved snippets (citations surfaced in UI).

### E) Dashboard UX (QuickSight)
- **Sheets**:  
  1) **Overview**: key indicators + recent releases.  
  2) **Forecasts**: quantile bands, scenario selection.  
  3) **Policy Path‑Finder**: goal entry, bundles list, explanation panel.  
  4) **Drilldowns**: BEA components (e.g., PCE by product) with interactions.  
QuickSight supports filters, drilldowns, actions, and exports (CSV/PDF), fitting hackathon usability goals. 

### F) Alerts
- **Deviation alerts**: EventBridge rule monitors forecast surprises and ingestion failures → **SNS** notifications; AWS docs provide step‑by‑step creation and custom subject lines via Lambda if needed. 
- **Model monitoring**: Clarify Feature Attribution Drift Monitor for continuous tracking; publishes metrics to CloudWatch for thresholds/alarms.

## 10) Governance, Trust & Responsible AI

- **Transparency**: Every policy bundle includes **forecast inputs, SHAP‑based explanations**, and retrieved **evidence** (RAG) to justify reasoning. Clarify documentation explains Shapley‑based feature attribution and its properties. 
- **Data freshness & revisions**: FRED’s vintage dates support “as‑of” views; UI can toggle latest vs. initial release to analyze sensitivity to revisions.

## 11) Success Metrics & Evaluation

- **Dashboard usability**: Task completion time to obtain a feasible policy bundle; number of interactions to convergence; qualitative user feedback.
- **Clarity of explanations**: Human rating of GenAI rationales (accuracy, clarity, concision) referencing SHAP/RAG artifacts; reduction in follow‑up questions.
- **Forecast quality**: Pinball loss across key series; calibration of quantile bands; backtests vs. last 8 quarters.
- **Policy feasibility**: Probability (from simulation) of meeting goals; robustness across stress scenarios (energy price shocks, FX shifts).

## 14) Risks & Mitigations

- **Forecast misspecification**: Use model ensembles (DeepAR + simple baselines) and backtesting; calibrate quantiles.
- **Policy realism**: Constrain actions; incorporate references (e.g., historical ranges).
- **LLM hallucination**: Strict guardrails, retrieval grounding, and citations in the UI. Bedrock guardrails/KBs reduce risk. 
- **Data revisions**: Offer vintage toggles; FRED vintages allow “as‑of” analysis.
