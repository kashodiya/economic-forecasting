## 0) Concept Summary — What makes this novel

Instead of only forecasting indicator curves, we **simulate how key economic actors respond to shocks in real time**. A swarm of lightweight AI agents (households, firms, banks, policymakers) ingest official macro data + summarized news, form expectations (LLM‑guided), and interact through markets and policy rules. The dashboard lets the user inject shocks (e.g., “energy price +20%,” “rate hike +50 bps”) and then **watch the mechanism**: agents update beliefs → change decisions → aggregate outcomes (inflation, GDP, unemployment) emerge. Forecasts are produced via AWS forecasting models, and **plain‑language explanations** are generated by the agents themselves (e.g., “households pulled back durables due to higher financing costs; banks tightened credit, amplifying the demand slowdown”).

Data sources are standard (FRED/BEA), but the novelty is:
- **Agentic simulation + ML forecasts in one loop**, not just static curves. 
- **LLM‑empowered agents** with calibrated expectations, memory, and negotiation over prices/wages/credit. 
- **Causal “what‑if” layer** (synthetic control) to compare shock paths vs counterfactuals. 
- **Explainability** (forecast drivers with SHAP/LIME‑style methods; AWS Forecast explainability; narrative rationales from agents). 
- **Alerting** rooted in modern anomaly detection patterns (matrix profile + Prophet) to avoid naïve thresholds.

## 1) Users & Success Metrics

**Primary users:** Economic Research team, macro strategists, and policy analysts.  
**Success metrics:**  
- **Usability/insights:** Time to scenario answer < 2 minutes; users can trace mechanism (“who caused what”) in ≤3 clicks.  
- **Clarity of AI explanations:** >80% of explanations rated “clear and actionable” in user testing.  
- **Forecast quality:** Backtested accuracy vs baselines (MAPE/Quantile pinball loss) + calibrated uncertainty bands.   
- **Alert relevance:** <10% false‑positive rate; alerts include root‑cause narrative and driver attributions.

## 2) Data & Ingestion (official, reliable, auditable)

**Sources:**  
- **FRED** (observations for inflation, unemployment, rates, etc.), via API endpoints (series/observations).   
- **BEA** (GDP, PCE, industry data), via BEA API (NIPA, GDP‑by‑industry, regional). 

**Design:**
- **Connector layer** pulls incremental updates on a schedule (e.g., hourly for series updates; daily for releases).  
- **Schema:** `indicator_id`, `vintage_date`, `obs_date`, `value`, `source`, `revision_flag`. FRED supports vintage dates; capture revisions for realism.   
- **Metadata & keys:** Secure storage of API keys for BEA; BEA datasets/parameters discoverable via API.   
- **Releases & provenance:** Track BEA/FRED release identifiers and table metadata for auditability. 

**Optional “news” stream for expectations formation:**  
- Summarize macro‑relevant headlines (oil, labor disputes, fiscal decisions) using instruction‑tuned LLMs with consistency checks (human‑validated approach shows instruction tuning matters more than size; use grounding to sources).

## 3) Core Architecture Overview

**Layers:**
1. **Data Lakehouse & Feature Store:** Persist FRED/BEA series, derived features (seasonal factors, gaps vs trend), and news embeddings.  
2. **Forecasting Services:** Managed **AWS Forecast** predictors + **SageMaker DeepAR** models for multi‑series forecasts and probabilistic outputs; output quantiles p10/p50/p90.   
3. **Agentic Digital Twin:** Swarm of LLM‑empowered agents representing households, firms, banks, policymakers; each agent receives signals + policies, updates beliefs, and acts.   
4. **Scenario & Causal Engine:** Inject shocks, compare with counterfactual trajectories using synthetic control variants; present deltas.   
5. **Explainability & Alerts:** SHAP/LIME‑style attributions for forecast drivers; anomaly detection (matrix profile + Prophet) triggers alerts with narratives.   
6. **Interactive UI:** Time‑aligned views (history, forecast, scenario paths), agent‑level rationales, deviation alerts, and “why” stories.

---

## 4) Forecasting Module (robust, probabilistic, explainable)

**Models:**
- **AWS Forecast** for automated pipelines and **Explainability** reports (impact scores of covariates—e.g., holidays, weather) + quantile forecasts.   
- **SageMaker DeepAR** for learning global patterns across many series (inflation subcomponents, sector indices), with p10/p50/p90 intervals and dynamic covariates (rates, oil).   
- **Model governance:** Backtesting with quantile loss rather than only MAPE; time‑based splits to avoid leakage; automated quality monitoring. 

**Explainability:**
- Combine AWS explainability with **SHAP for time series** to show which lags/covariates drove forecasts; visualize horizon‑specific feature impact.   
- Add **plain‑English rationales** on top (generated by an LLM but grounded in SHAP attributes and recent data snippets). 

---

## 5) Agentic Digital Twin — how it operates

**Agent classes & state:**
- **Households:** Income, savings, credit access, expectations of inflation, employment; decide consumption (durables/nondurables/services) and labor supply.  
- **Firms:** Productivity, inventories, cost of capital, demand expectations; set prices/wages, investment, hiring.  
- **Banks:** Balance sheets, capital ratios, funding costs; set lending standards, credit volumes, deposit rates.  
- **Policymakers (CB/Govt):** Policy rules (Taylor‑style heuristic; fiscal automatic stabilizers), discretionary shocks.

**Agent cognition (LLM‑empowered):**
- Each agent has **perception** (FRED/BEA signals + news summaries), **short/long‑term memory**, and **planning** modules. Expectations are *calibrated* to official data, with LLMs generating narratives and preferences subject to constraints (e.g., budget, policy rules).   
- Agents **negotiate** over prices/wages/credit—e.g., firms propose wages given inflation expectations; households respond with reservation wages; banks adjust credit spreads given default risk.  
- **Alignment:** Incorporate ABM literature heuristics (bounded rationality, learning), and use simple market‑clearing routines to derive aggregate outputs. 

**Calibration & validation:**
- Calibrate agents to match stylized facts (Phillips‑curve‑like relationships, inventory cycles, credit procyclicality). Use ABM validation practices (target moments, distributional checks). 

**Novelty in “what‑if”:**
- When the user injects a shock (e.g., oil +20%), agents update beliefs (news + data) → revise decisions → emergent macro path appears. This is **interpretable** because you can inspect **agents’ narratives** (“banks tightened standards due to higher default risk; firms postponed capex”), and link them to SHAP drivers on the forecasted series. 

---

## 6) Scenario & Causal Layer (credible counterfactuals)

**Mechanics:**
- For each scenario, the system generates **two trajectories**:
  1) **Twin path** with agent interactions + ML forecasts under the shock.
  2) **Counterfactual** using **Synthetic Control** (and time‑aware SC variants), estimated on pre‑shock donors (e.g., similar periods or related geographies/industries) to approximate “no shock.” 

**Outcome comparison:**
- Show Δ GDP, Δ CPI, Δ unemployment between twin vs counterfactual, with uncertainty bands and a **causal explanation** (“most of the gap is explained by tightened credit and durables demand collapse”). 

---

## 7) Alerts & Monitoring (robust, low‑noise)

**Why alerts fire:**  
- **Significant deviations**: An observed indicator leaves forecast bands (probabilistic), or matrix‑profile discord indicates a new regime/pattern.   
- **Drivers & narratives:** Each alert includes SHAP‑based driver attribution (“energy subindex accounted for +0.4pp”) and an agent narrative explaining the mechanism (“firms passed through costs rapidly in oligopolistic segments”).   
- **Prophet‑based guardrails:** Use Prophet components for seasonality/holiday decomposition to avoid false positives on known cycles.

## 8) UI/UX — make the mechanism visible

**Key views:**
1) **Trends & Forecasts:** Multiseries panel; toggle between p10/p50/p90; show explainability overlays (feature importance over horizon).   
2) **Agent Theater:** Card grid showing each agent’s **state** (beliefs, constraints) and **action** (consumption, pricing, lending), with short natural‑language rationales.   
3) **Scenario Studio:** Left pane to apply shocks (e.g., oil, policy, credit); right pane shows resulting macro path vs synthetic‑control counterfactual; timeline scrubber to replay agent interactions.   
4) **Alerts & “Why” Feed:** Chronological stream of deviations; each item bundles forecast band breach, driver attribution, and agent narrative.   
5) **Release & Revision Tracker:** Small panel tracking FRED/BEA releases (with links to source tables) to keep analysts grounded in official updates.

## 9) Governance, Reliability, and Safety

- **Source reliability:** All macro facts are from FRED/BEA; each explanation references the underlying series IDs and release tables (auditable).   
- **LLM news summarization:** Use instruction‑tuned models and **consistency checks**; prioritize grounding in source text to reduce hallucinations, consistent with news summarization research findings.   
- **ABM realism & bias controls:** Calibrate agent priors to match stylized facts; limit agent freedom via constraints (budget identity, accounting consistency); periodically compare simulated aggregates to official data.   
- **Explainability:** Prefer **model‑agnostic SHAP/LIME** and AWS explainability features; show uncertainty explicitly; avoid over‑confident language.

## 10) Technical Blueprint (systems view)

**Data pipeline:**  
- Scheduled ingestion from FRED API (`series/observations`—supports JSON/CSV) + BEA API (NIPA/GDP tables). Persist with versioned metadata (vintage dates). 

**Forecast infra:**  
- **AWS Forecast** for end‑to‑end automation & explainability; **SageMaker DeepAR** for custom multi‑series learning; both emit probabilistic forecasts and evaluation metrics. 

**Agent platform:**  
- Containerized microservices per agent type; each service has a **belief module** (LLM + memory), **decision module** (rules/heuristics + constraints), and **interaction interface** (market clearing). Architectures follow recent LLM‑agent ABM patterns (perception, memory, planning, reflection). 

**Scenario engine:**  
- Shock DSL (domain‑specific schema: variable, magnitude, duration); a **causal estimator** computes counterfactual paths using **Synthetic Control / Time‑Aware SC**, backed by pre‑shock donor pools. 

**Explainability & alerts:**  
- SHAP pipeline tied to forecast features; anomaly service combining **matrix profile** (shape anomalies) and **Prophet** residual bands. 

**UI:**  
- Web dashboard with stateful scenario sessions; time‑aligned charts; agent rationales as expandable cards; source citations included with each explanation.

## 11) Example User Journey (end‑to‑end)

1) Kaushik opens **Scenario Studio**, selects **“Energy price shock: +20%, next 2 quarters”**.  
2) Dashboard runs:
   - Forecast module updates CPI components with new covariate path (probabilistic intervals).   
   - Agents ingest shock: **firms** raise prices where pass‑through is feasible; **households** cut durable purchases; **banks** tighten lending for autos. Narratives are generated and shown.   
   - Synthetic control builds a counterfactual “no‑shock” CPI/GDP path; UI overlays Δ paths.   
3) Kaushik clicks **“Explain”**:
   - SHAP summary shows **energy contribution +0.6pp** to CPI; **credit conditions** drive −0.4pp GDP in Q+2.   
   - Agents’ rationales link to series and news snippets (grounded).   
4) **Alerts** fire when realized CPI prints exceed p90; the alert includes *driver breakdown + agent narrative*.

## 12) Validation Plan

- **Backtests:** Rolling‑origin evaluation for major indicators; track pinball loss at target quantiles; monitor drift.   
- **ABM sanity checks:** Stylized facts (e.g., inflation‑output trade‑off) emerge under plausible parameter ranges.   
- **Causal layer tests:** Placebo tests (apply synthetic control where no shock happened) and donor pool sensitivity analyses.   
- **Explanation clarity:** User studies rate narratives for clarity and actionability; iterate prompts and grounding per summarization research.

## 13) Roadmap (phased, achievable within a hackathon)

**Phase 0 (Day 1–2):**  
- Wire up FRED/BEA ingestion; minimal schema with vintage capture.   
- Baseline AWS Forecast predictor for 2–3 series (CPI headline, GDP growth, unemployment). 

**Phase 1 (Day 3–4):**  
- Simple **agents** with calibrated heuristics (no RL): households adjust consumption; firms adjust pricing; banks adjust lending thresholds.   
- **Scenario** controls and **synthetic control** counterfactual for one indicator (CPI). 

**Phase 2 (Day 5–6):**  
- Add **DeepAR** for multi‑series; integrate **SHAP** explainability and matrix‑profile/Prophet alerts.   
- Build **Agent Theater** UI with rationales grounded in data/news summaries. 

**Stretch:**  
- Expand to **banks’ policy** (capital/rate shocks), **policymaker agent** with Taylor‑style rule; add **Time‑Aware Synthetic Control** for stronger trend settings.

## 14) Why judges will find this compelling

- **Mechanism transparency:** Not just forecasts, but **why** outcomes changed (agent narratives + SHAP drivers).   
- **Causal rigor:** Counterfactual with synthetic control—bridges the gap between scenario play and credible policy evaluation.   
- **Alert quality:** Modern anomaly detection reduces noise and explains root causes.   
- **Auditability:** Everything ties back to official FRED/BEA series and release tables.

## 15) Risks & Mitigations

- **LLM hallucinations:** Use instruction‑tuned models, strict grounding to data/news; include citations and confidence flags.   
- **Agent calibration complexity:** Start with literature‑informed heuristics; validate against stylized facts; avoid over‑fitting.   
- **Causal misinterpretation:** Present counterfactual uncertainty bands; document donor selection and placebo tests.
